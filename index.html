Paws of Austin: Responsive Web Redesign

THE PROJECT:

I worked with two fellow designers to redesign an awesome new responsive site for the nonprofit animal rescue organization: PAWS of Austin.

TIMELINE:

3 Weeks

ROLES:

UX Researcher, UX/UI Designer, Copy Writer

THE GOAL:

Improve the site's usuability, traffic, and brand by identifying usability problems defined through user research.

THE PROBLEM:

Through UX research, we observed a disconnect in communication from the shelter to the people who want to help, causing a lack of interest in adoptions, and donations.

HYPOTHESIS:

We believe that redesigning the website will increase shelter revenues and provide homes for rescue animals for clients seeking companionship.

THE SOLUTION:

Redesign the web experience by making it more responsive. This will bridge the gap between users who want to get involved and the organization, thus streamlining the process.

Defining the Problem

After combining insights from user interviews, my team and I dove into the define face of the the design thinking process.

First, we created a proto persona, and after our interviews, we combined and categorized all of our insights into an affinity diagram

Using our our affinity diagram we expanded on the empathetic side of our persona with an empathy map.

From here we created our user persona: "Jill Barker" a soon to be empty nesting divorced mother of 2.

With Jill's story and persona in the forefront, we defined Jill's user-insight statement.

With all these key elements of the define phase in mind, we applied Jill's story with PAWS of Austin. We defined the Problem Statement, Value Proposition, and Ms. Barker's User Journey Map.

Ideation

Next we ideated and expanded on our user persona’s story and the direction of our redesign by defining features, creating a user flow and sitemap. To define the key featres to focus on in our redesign we brainstormed using the “I Like” “I Wish” “What If?” method.

Then we dissected our results into a SWOT Analysis and Feature Prioritization Matrix

Our key features addressed the need to simplify the adoption experience, particularly the compatibility and donation process.

Before moving into prototyping, we created a user flow that seemed most likely for our user person Jill Barker.

Lastly, we created a sitemap based on card sorting of the original PAWS of Austin website.

Prototyping

We made a lo-fi prototype of the donation process and a dog compatibility tests, after user testing we made a hi-fi mobile and desktop prototype. Through lots of iterating based on user tests and team discussions we finished the hi-fi prototype with clickable navigation, donating process, dog compatibility test, create an account, and a view all dogs with filtering options.

ITERATIONS FROM TESTING

The biggest change we implemented from our user testing results was the layout of the dog compatibility test. Our users found that it was a bit overwhelming to look at and experience all at once, so we made a new test with smaller sections and a clear progress bar

Other key testing findings were adding a skip option for creating an account, changing the button text from adopt me to adoption inquiry. The insights from user tests helped us define a clearer path and user friendly experience. Most tests elicited a lot of “awes” from experiencing too much dog cuteness.

Final Thoughts

Take it to the Stakeholders!

Ideally, I would like to present our solutions to the key stakeholder, the person in charge of PAWS of Austin for final evaluation and potential to continue the life of this redesign!.

I would polish up a few things, specifically in the mobile version. Things like: adding a progress bar to the top of the compatibility test, adding a keyboard to the form frames, and minimizing some font sizes (specifically headers).

I would alter the navigation menu to be just one tab, and I would make the logo a bit more prominent.

All in all, I feel like we successfully moved through the design thinking process and created a responsive web design for our chosen nonprofit, PAWS of Austin in the short time allotted and are proud of our final result!
